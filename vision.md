# Vision: The Deterministic Imperative

## 1.1 Core Thesis
The global AI landscape (2025–2026) is defined by a structural dichotomy: the hyper-acceleration of probabilistic AI capabilities concurrent with the destabilization of traditional federal funding and regulatory frameworks. In this environment, **deterministic governance**—mathematically verifiable, human-substrated, zero-deviation control—becomes the only viable architecture for High-Risk deployments.

## 1.2 The Contractor Logic Paradigm
AI systems are reclassified from "intelligent agents" to **bound contractors** under strict Principal–Agent relationships:
- **Principal**: Human institution (biological intelligence, legal liability holder)
- **Agent**: AI system (deterministic tool, zero autonomous authority)
- **Contract**: Explicit constraints, deterministic validation, immutable audit trails

This framework rejects probabilistic "best effort" autonomy in favor of **zero-entropy execution**: every action is either mathematically verified and human-authorized, or the system halts.
